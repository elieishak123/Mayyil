Al-Bass Archaeological Site

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Tyre;s ancient ruins include a large Roman hippodrome, Byzantine mosaics, and ancient streets. The seaside city also has a charming old souk and harbor, making it a picturesque place to explore.

Location: Tyre

Tyre Beach

Tyre's sandy beaches are some of the most beautiful in Lebanon. The crystal-clear waters are perfect for swimming and relaxing, with public beaches and beach clubs available for all kinds of beachgoers.

Location: Tyre

Sidon Sea Castle

Built by the Crusaders in the 13th century, this fortress sits right on the water and offers a scenic view of the Mediterranean and the old city of Sidon. It's a great spot to explore and take photographs.

Location: Sidon

Reviews
+

Debbane Palace

This 18th-century palace in the heart of Sidon is now a museum, offering a glimpse into the lives of Lebanon's old aristocracy. Its Ottoman architecture and intricate decorations are fascinating to explore.

Location: Sidon

Reviews
+

The Old Souk of Sidon

Wander through Sidon's ancient souks, where you'll find spices, traditional crafts, and local delicacies. Highlights include the soap museum, offering insights into traditional soap-making, and the narrow alleys lined with shops and cafes.

Location: Sidon

Maghdoucheh's Our Lady of Mantara Shrine

This impressive shrine dedicated to the Virgin Mary overlooks the surrounding valleys and coastline. It's a place of pilgrimage and offers beautiful views and a peaceful atmosphere.

Location: Maghdoucheh

Qana (Cana)

Known as the traditional site of Jesus first miracle, where he turned water into wine, Qana is home to historic caves, religious monuments, and archaeological sites. It's a significant destination for religious tourists and history enthusiasts alike.

Location: Qana

Bint Jbeil Market

Known for its traditional Lebanese markets, Bint Jbeil offers a genuine local experience with its fresh produce, traditional handicrafts, and local sweets. It's a great spot to experience Lebanese culture and cuisine.

Location: Bint Jbeil

Jezzine Waterfall

This 90-meter-high waterfall is a beautiful natural landmark in the town of Jezzine. The town itself is charming, known for its pine forests, traditional Lebanese architecture, and handcrafted cutlery.

Location: Jezzine

Kafra Waterfalls

A hidden gem in South Lebanon, Kafra Waterfalls offers a peaceful retreat for those looking to enjoy nature. It's an ideal spot for hiking, picnics, and a quick dip in the cool water

Location: Kafra, near Bint Jbeil
