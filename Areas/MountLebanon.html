Discover Mount Lebanon

+

Jeita Grotto

this breathtaking limestone cave system features stunning stalactites and stalagmites. Visitors can take a boat ride in the lower grotto, which is often considered one of the most beautiful in the world.

Location: Jeita

Reviews
+

Harissa

Located on a hill overlooking Jounieh, Harissa is famous for the towering statue of Our Lady of Lebanon. You can take a cable car from Jounieh up to Harissa for panoramic views of the Mediterranean and the surrounding area.

Location: Jounieh

Reviews
+

Byblos (Jbeil)

One of the world's oldest continuously inhabited cities, Byblos is filled with ancient ruins, including a Crusader castle, Phoenician temples, and old souks. The coastal town is also home to beautiful beaches, waterfront cafes, and a picturesque harbor.

Location: Byblos District

Reviews
+

Faqra Ruins

This archaeological site features Roman temples, altars, and a large tower amid a mountainous landscape. It's particularly beautiful in the winter and is close to Lebanon's main ski resorts.

Location: Kfardebian

Reviews
+

Baatara Gorge Waterfall

This spectacular waterfall plunges 255 meters into a limestone cave known as the “Three Bridges Chasm.” The waterfall is most impressive during spring when the snowmelt swells the flow.

Location: Tannourine

Reviews
+

Deir el Qamar

A picturesque village with well-preserved traditional Lebanese houses, Deir el Qamar was once the capital of Mount Lebanon. It's known for its charming squares, churches, and the Emir's Palace, as well as its vibrant cultural festivals.

Location: Chouf District

Reviews
+

Beiteddine Palace

This 19th-century palace built by Emir Bashir Shihab II is a masterpiece of Lebanese architecture. Its grand courtyards, mosaic floors, and ornate rooms are a window into Lebanon's rich history and heritage.

Location: Chouf District

Reviews
+

Mzaar Kfardebian Ski Resort

Lebanon's largest ski resort and a popular winter destination, Mzaar offers a range of slopes for skiers and snowboarders, along with beautiful mountain views. In summer, it's great for hiking and mountain biking.

Location: Kfardebian

Reviews
+

Chouwen Lake

Nestled in the Jabal Moussa Biosphere Reserve, Chouwen Lake is a stunning blue-green lake surrounded by lush forests. It's ideal for hiking, picnicking, and swimming, with trails leading through beautiful landscapes.

Location: Chouwen, Keserwan

Reviews
+

Afqa Grotto

According to legend, the birthplace of the Greek goddess Aphrodite, this waterfall flows out of a massive cave and is surrounded by beautiful, rugged landscapes. It's especially beautiful in spring.

Location: Afqa, Jbeil District

Reviews
+

Jabal Moussa Biosphere Reserve

A UNESCO Biosphere Reserve with stunning biodiversity, Jabal Moussa offers a variety of hiking trails that lead through forests, cliffs, and ancient ruins, providing excellent views over the Mediterranean.

Location: Keserwan District

Mar Sarkis Monastery (Saint Sergius and Bacchus Monastery)

An ancient monastery with beautiful views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. It's a quiet place for spiritual reflection and exploration of old Christian architecture.

Location: Maad, Byblos District

Ain Zhalta Cedars Reserve

A beautiful natural reserve within the Shouf Cedar Nature Reserve, the largest in Lebanon. Ain Zhalta offers beautiful hiking trails through cedar forests, home to diverse flora and fauna.
