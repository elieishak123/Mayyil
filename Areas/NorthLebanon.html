Tripoli Old Souks

Explore Tripoli's historic old souks, where you can wander through bustling markets selling everything from spices and textiles to sweets and traditional crafts. Highlights include the Khan al-Saboun (Soap Khan) and the Tailor's Souk, each with its own unique atmosphere.

Location: Tripoli

Reviews
+

Citadel of Raymond de Saint-Gilles

Also known as Tripoli Castle, this 12th-century Crusader fortress offers a look into Lebanon's medieval past and provides panoramic views over Tripoli and the Mediterranean coast. It's one of the largest and best-preserved Crusader castles in the region.

Location: Tripoli

Reviews
+

Mseilha Fort

A small yet scenic fortress perched on a rocky hill overlooking the Mseilha Valley. Built in the 17th century to guard trade routes, the fort offers beautiful views of the surrounding landscape.

Location: Chekka, Batroun District

Reviews

Batroun

This coastal town combines beautiful beaches with historic sites like Phoenician sea walls, ancient churches, and charming old streets filled with cafes and boutiques. It's also famous for its lemonade!

Location: Batroun District

Reviews
+

Qadisha Valley

This UNESCO World Heritage site is a deep gorge filled with ancient monasteries, churches, and hermitages carved into rock walls. It's a great place for history lovers and hikers alike, offering breathtaking scenery.

Location: Bsharri District

Reviews
+

Cedars of God (Al Arz)

The iconic cedar forests of Lebanon are a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Visitors can enjoy walking through the ancient cedar trees, some of which are over a thousand years old, and visit the nearby Cedars Ski Resort in winter.

Location: Bsharri

Reviews
+

Gibran Museum

Dedicated to the Lebanese-American writer and philosopher Kahlil Gibran, the museum houses his manuscripts, paintings, and personal belongings. Located in Gibran's hometown of Bsharri, the museum offers insight into his life and works

Location: Bsharri

Reviews
+

Ras Al-Chaqa'a (The Cliff of Chekka)

This dramatic cliff area features beautiful sea caves and is a popular spot for adventurous activities like cliff diving and snorkeling. The waters here are crystal clear, making it perfect for swimming and exploring underwater.

Location: Chekka

Reviews
+

Saydet el Nourieh Monastery

A cliffside monastery dedicated to Our Lady of Light, with stunning views over the Mediterranean. It's a serene place of worship and offers a peaceful retreat for visitors looking to relax and reflect.

Location: Chekka

Reviews
+

Bnachii Lake

A popular spot for families and nature lovers, Bnachii Lake offers paddle boating, picnicking, and surrounding restaurants. The lake is surrounded by pine forests and offers a beautiful escape into nature.

Location: Zgharta District

Reviews
+

Kfarhelda Waterfalls

These stunning waterfalls are perfect for nature lovers and photographers. The area is ideal for picnicking and cooling off in natural pools during the summer.

Location: Batroun District
