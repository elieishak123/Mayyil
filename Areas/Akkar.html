Qammouaa Forest

one of Lebanon's largest and oldest cedar forests. The forest also includes oak, juniper, and fir trees. There are trails perfect for hiking, picnicking, and enjoying the natural beauty of the mountains. The Qammouaa area is stunning year-round, with snow in winter and lush greenery in summer.

Location: Qammouaa, Akkar District

+

Akkar Al-Atiqa

This ancient town has traditional stone houses, winding alleys, and historic landmarks. There are churches and mosques dating back centuries, making it an ideal place to experience traditional Lebanese architecture and culture.

Location: Akkar Al-Atiqa Village, Akkar

Reviews
+

Oyoun Al-Samak

Known for its natural springs and rivers, Oyoun Al-Samak is a favorite spot for locals to picnic and relax. The area is surrounded by mountains and greenery, and visitors can enjoy fresh fish at nearby restaurants.

Location: Between the villages of Meshmesh and Beit El Faqs, Akkar

Reviews
+

Mount Akroum

Mount Akroum is a popular site for bird watching and has a variety of migratory birds passing through the area. The views from the top are also magnificent, with vistas of nearby valleys and villages.

Location: Akroum, Akkar

Reviews
+

Wadi Jhannam(Valley of Hell)

Despite its name, Wadi Jhannam is a beautiful valley with unique rock formations, small waterfalls, and natural springs. It's perfect for adventurous travelers looking to hike in a wild and rugged landscape.

Location: Near Fnaideq, Akkar District

Reviews
+

Tour the Small Village of Mish Mish

Known for its apricot trees, Mish Mish is a quaint village with authentic Lebanese rural vibes. You can interact with locals, learn about their agricultural practices, and enjoy the simple, peaceful village life.

Location: Mish Mish, Akkar District
