Baalbek Roman Temples (Baalbek Ruins)

This UNESCO World Heritage Site is one of Lebanon's most iconic landmarks, featuring the Temple of Bacchus, the Temple of Jupiter, and the Temple of Venus. These colossal ruins date back to the Roman Empire and are incredibly well-preserved.

Location: Baalbek City Center

Baalbek Museum

This ancient town has traditional stone houses, winding alleys, and historic landmarks. There are churches and mosques dating back centuries, making it an ideal place to experience traditional Lebanese architecture and culture.

Location: Akkar Al-Atiqa Village, Akkar

Stone of the Pregnant Woman (Hajar al-Hibla)

Known as one of the world's largest ancient stone blocks, this megalith weighs an estimated 1,000 tons and was likely cut for use in the Temple of Jupiter.

Location: Quarry outside Baalbek, along the road leading to the main temple complex

Baalbek International Festival (Seasonal)

This annual summer festival is held in the temple ruins and features performances by international and Lebanese artists. The festival offers an unforgettable experience of music, dance, and art within the dramatic backdrop of the ancient temples.

Location: Baalbek Temple Complex

Baalbek's Old Souks

Baalbek's old souks are full of character, with shops selling local handicrafts, spices, textiles, and traditional Lebanese sweets. Walking through the souks gives a taste of Baalbek's rich culture and an opportunity to buy unique souvenirs.

Location: Old Market Area, Baalbek City Center

Reviews
+

Sayyida Khawla Shrine

This shrine, dedicated to Sayyida Khawla (daughter of Imam Hussein), is a beautiful and peaceful place of worship. The mosque has stunning Islamic architecture, intricate tilework, and a tranquil atmosphere, making it a notable spiritual site in Baalbek.

Location: Near the entrance of Baalbek, along the main road

Reviews
+

Umayyad Mosque (Great Mosque of Baalbek)

Built during the Umayyad period, one of the oldest mosques in Lebanon. It features early Islamic architecture and is built with stones from ancient temples, giving it a unique blend of Roman and Islamic styles.

Location: Near the Baalbek Ruins in Baalbek City Center

Ras El Ein Park

popular green space in Baalbek, named after the Ras El Ein spring that supplies water to the park and surrounding areas. This park is a refreshing destination within Baalbek and provides a pleasant contrast to the ancient ruins and historical sites the city is known for.

Location: Ras El Ein Park, Baalbek, Lebanon
