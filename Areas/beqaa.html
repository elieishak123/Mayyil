Anjar Archaeological Site

This UNESCO World Heritage site features well-preserved ruins from the 8th-century Umayyad dynasty. It is an excellent example of early Islamic architecture, showcasing arcades, palaces, and ancient streets. Anjar also has a rich Armenian heritage, so don't miss the local Armenian bakeries and cultural shops nearby.

Location: Anjar

Lake Qaroun

Lake Qaroun is a large artificial lake perfect for a leisurely day out. It offers boat rentals, peaceful picnic spots, and lakeside cafes where you can try fresh fish dishes and other Lebanese cuisine. Aamiq Wetlands

The Aamiq Wetlands are Lebanon's largest remaining natural wetlands, a sanctuary for various migratory birds and other wildlife. Take a guided eco-tour or walk the wooden trails through marshes, fields, and forests. It's an ideal spot for nature lovers and photographers.

Location: Aamiq, near Lake Qaroun

Deir el Ahmar's Agricultural Sites

Known for its beautiful landscapes and agriculture, Deir el Ahmar offers visitors a glimpse into local farming life. The area is renowned for its apple orchards, vineyards, and pomegranate groves. The nearby St. Joseph's Monastery offers a panoramic view of the valley.

Location: Deir el Ahmar, northern Beqaa

Nature of Taanayel

Taanayel is a serene agricultural village known for the Taanayel Lake and the Taanayel Monastery farm. Visitors can stroll around the lake, rent bicycles, and try local dairy products like yogurt and labneh. The area is popular for picnics and a peaceful escape into nature.

Location: Taanayel, near Chtaura
